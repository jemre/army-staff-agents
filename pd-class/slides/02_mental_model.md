# Section 2 — Core Mental Model: How LLMs "Think"
*5 minutes*

---

## Slide: What It Is Not

AI is **not** a search engine.
AI is **not** a magic 8-ball.
AI is **not** omniscient.

---

## Slide: The Analogy

**Think about the best staff officer you've ever worked with.**

Day one, they show up knowing:
- Doctrine ✓
- How staff work functions ✓
- How to write an OPORD ✓

But they don't know:
- Your unit ✗
- Your commander's priorities ✗
- The current mission ✗
- Your format preferences ✗

---

## Slide: Day One vs. Day 30

**Day one:** "Write me a risk assessment."

→ Technically correct. Operationally useless.

**After a proper brief:** Same officer, same question.

→ Your format. Your thresholds. Your situation.

**AI works exactly the same way.**

---

## Slide: The Core Insight

> The quality of the output is almost entirely
> a function of the quality of the input.

AI draws on everything it was trained on
and applies it to what **you give it.**

Give it nothing → it guesses.
Give it the right context → it advises.

---

## Slide: The Mental Model

**Stop thinking:** "How do I get AI to do this?"

**Start thinking:** "How do I brief this staff officer?"

- What role do I need them in?
- What do they need to know?
- What do I need back, and in what format?

That's prompt and context engineering.
