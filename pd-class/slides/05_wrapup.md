# Section 5 — Wrapping Up
*5 minutes*

---

## Slide: The Three Things

**1. AI is a staff officer, not a search engine.**
It needs to be briefed.
Treat it like a capable but uninformed advisor —
give it role, task, context, and format.

**2. Prompt engineering is how you ask.**
**Context engineering is what you bring.**
Master both and you have a force multiplier.
Skip both and you get outputs you'll throw away.

**3. Start with one real task this week.**
Not everything. One thing.
An award citation. A talking points memo.
A training schedule. A risk assessment.
Apply what you learned. See what happens.

---

## Slide: The Mental Model, One More Time

> "Brief it like you'd brief a new staff officer."

- **Role** — Who are you talking to?
- **Task** — What specifically do you need?
- **Context** — What does it need to know?
- **Format** — How should it come back to you?

That's it.

---

## Slide: What This Is Not

AI will not:
- Replace your judgment
- Own the product
- Know what classified means
- Go on the ground
- Be held accountable

**You are still the staff officer.**
AI is the tool that helps you produce better products faster.

---

## Slide: Where This Is Going

*(Optional — use if the room is engaged and you have time)*

What we covered today is the foundation.

The next level:
- **Agents** — AI that coordinates with other AI to build multi-section staff products
- **Persistent memory** — AI that knows your unit, your SOPs, your commander's priorities
- **Automated products** — Slide decks and documents generated from AI outlines

Units that build this capability now will have a significant advantage.
